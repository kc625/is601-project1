    How to Solve the Telephone's Fatal Flaw

    Switchboards and the Nuclear Bomb

    In the early 20th century, telephones required an analog connection through a circuit. Centralized switchboard locations, like the one seen here, existed in order to connect people's phone calls to the correct place. With World War II came the creation of missile bombings and the nuclear bomb. A government realization came that this posed a huge problem. A switchboard center could be a target for a bombing, which in turn would cripple the entire country's communication system in one fell swoop!
